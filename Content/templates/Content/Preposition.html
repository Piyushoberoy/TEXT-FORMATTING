What is Preposition?

Preposition is a word or phrase that connects a noun or pronoun to a verb or adjective in a sentence. They are usually short words, and they normally are found in front of nouns. On rare occasions, prepositions can be found in front of gerund verbs.

TYPES OF PREPOSITIONS
Simple preposition:
Simple prepositions are the short words used to show the relationship between nouns, pronouns, or to join parts of a clause or sentence. Examples:-
  • The dog jumped out while the car was moving.
  • Dorothy came upon the Tin Man.
Double Preposition:
Easily form a Double Prepositions by joining two simple prepositions.. Examples:-
  • The dog jumped out of the movingcar.
  • The child hid inside of the cabinet.
Compound Preposition:
Compound prepositions are those prepositions that are made by prefixing the preposition to a noun, an adjective or an adverb. Say for example, amongst, amidst, above, around, along, across, about, below, beneath, beside, between, beyond, outside, within, without.. Examples:-
  • The cat is jumping around the seat.
  • The car pulled along the driveway.
Participle Preposition:
Verbs that act as a preposition. Usually, end in -ing and -ed.. Examples:-
  • Barring a few minor mistakes, the play went pretty good.
  • Everyone, please keep quiet during the class.during the class.
Phrase preposition:
A Phrase preposition includes a preposition, an object and the object’s modifier. Examples:-
  • My little brother collected my trophy on behalf of my sister.
  • In order to avoid losses, he withdrew all money to mitigate the risk.
Disguised preposition:
Disguised prepositions are those prepositions which are not used in the sentences directly, but we use them in a disguised way. Their shorter forms are used.
Examples:-
  • The ceremony will be held at 5 o’ clock.
  • We all went to a party
